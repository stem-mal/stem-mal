Qatar And Arab World Crises


Our Arab societies posses a great past, majestic glory, prestigious history, substantial victories, virtuous minds, sonventional paths

A civilization whose essence is Islam , whose leader is the greatest of mankind, whose statement is to never look behind ..

The origin of scientists .. the birth place of knowledge .. the destination of Muslims till the judgment day..

A civilization that arose, and subsequently .. the authentic history ..


The dawn of the Arab-Islamic civilization arose in the beginnings of the eighth century AD until the fifteenth century AD, and its distinction in religious, scientific, intellectual, and philosophical fields was between the middle of the ninth century AD and the twelfth century AD in what is known as the Islamic golden age, it was the origin of skillful scholars, eloquent writers, and shrewd pioneers.

But civilizations are like a living organism in its cycle. As all civilizations begin with the stage of formation, then maturity and prosperity, then weakness and extinction. Often the reason behind this refraction is the internal weakness and disintegration, and the abundance of conflicts, oppositions, civil wars, class division, and so on. In these harsh ages, the great importance of countries supporting each other and rising to solve their pressing issues materialize to us. As they are united by one history, religion and language.

Some Arab countries, such as Syria, Sudan, and Palestine, have faced great problems and difficulties, but they have found support from their Arab brothers, who always strive to uphold the word of the Arabs, preserve their unity, and overcome their complications.

Qatar is one of the strongest Arab countries internally and externally, which shows its permanent support for the brotherly Arab countries, and follows their footsteps in confronting the enemy, achieving peace, security and stability, and it has had - over the decades - wise opinions regarding the most prominent problems of the Arab world, which we will present below..

1. Viewpoint on the Palestinian issue:

Qatar's position on the Palestinian issue is among the strongest and strongest in the Arab world. Historically, Qatar has played an important role in providing political, economic, and humanitarian assistance to all Palestinians for many years. These positions are based on shared values of moral humanity, upholding the resilience of the Palestinian people, upholding their legitimate rights, most importantly the establishment of their independent state with Jerusalem as its capital Qatar was one of the first Arab states to open an office of the Palestinian Liberation Organization, which later became an embassy when the state of Palestine was declared at the Algiers Conference in 1988. Emir continues to emphasize Qatar's Viewpoint in support of the Palestinian cause and people, and strongly advocates an end to the Israeli occupation of the Arabs lands -continues to provide humanitarian assistance.

While many Arab governments have ignored the issue, Qatar has voiced its demands at international and UN forums, calling for an end to the occupation, a strong international stance against excesses do not cease settlement activity and prevent the occupation of Jerusalem. Compared to other Arab and Islamic states supporting this cause, Qatar's role is historically significant. Qatar played a key role in the signing of the Doha Accords between Palestinian factions on February 6, 2012, whether Fatah or Hamas, which agreed to form a unity government and presidential and parliamentary elections Procedures for national reconciliation will play a role in Qatar There have also been ongoing communication between the parties to develop.

Furthermore, Qatar consistently expresses its complete rejection of any measures that call for recognizing Jerusalem as the capital of Israel, as such actions undermine international efforts aimed at implementing the two-state solution. In the same context, Doha consistently emphasizes its rejection of plans to Judaize Jerusalem, alter its status, or relocate any embassy there. Qatar also opposes Israeli settlement activities in all forms and shapes. In addition to government aid during the period 2010-2016, Qatar provided nearly 1.345 billion Qatari Riyals in non-governmental assistance.

These substantial funds were directed towards humanitarian and developmental sectors, healthcare, social welfare, employee salaries, and funding agencies engaged in humanitarian aid. Qatar also contributed 50 million US dollars to the United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees (UNRWA) in June 2018 to support the education sector, enabling UNRWA to open its schools without delay at the start of the academic year.

Furthermore, His Highness Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani, the Amir of the State of Qatar, ordered the immediate provision of assistance totaling 150 million US dollars to support Palestinians in Gaza and secure the necessary fuel for electricity generation. This Qatari grant comes after more than three years of waiting since Qatar announced the establishment of a fund for the reconstruction of the Gaza Strip, pledging a quarter of a billion dollars during the Arab Economic Summit held in Kuwait in January 2009, directly after the Zionist aggression on the Gaza Strip.

2. Viewpoint on the famine in Yemen:

Qatar has played a significant role in alleviating the Yemeni famine. Qatar has supported peaceful solutions that ensure Yemen's security, stability and territorial integrity. It has for called the engagement of all stakeholders in the Yemeni scene with the support of the international community in a comprehensive political process and national reconciliation. These efforts aim to fulfill the aspirations of the Yemeni people for security, stability, and prosperity. Qatar welcomed the United Nations' announcement of a two-month extension of the ceasefire in Yemen, expressing hope that this extension will pave the way for a peaceful resolution of the Yemeni crisis and the realization of the Yemeni people's aspirations for security peace, and development.

Additionally, Qatar has reported U.S. sanctions. $100 million for humanitarian activities in Yemen. A portion of this money will be donated to the World Food Program, underscoring the importance of funding to end hunger in Yemen and save millions of lives. In a statement, Mr. Beasley stressed Qatar's support, which includes the intervention of all stakeholders in the situation in Yemen supported by the international community, to a comprehensive political process and national reconciliation aims to fulfill the aspirations of the Yemeni people for security and stability and prosperity. Qatar welcomed the UN announcement to extend the ceasefire in Yemen for two months, expressing hope that the extension would pave the way for a peaceful solution to the Yemeni crisis and the security, peace, and development of the Yemeni people.

The World Food Program supports 12.9 million people in Yemen with emergency food assistance and provides specialized nutrition for the treatment and prevention of malnutrition to 3.3 million children and mothers.

Qatar has been working on divisions in the Sudanese military and has called for an immediate ceasefire between rival factions in Sudan. Qatar has insisted on resolving disputes through dialogue. The call came in consideration of the conflict between the Sudanese Army and the "Rapid Support Force". Qatar's Ministry of Foreign Affairs has expressed grave concern over the developing situation in Khartoum and Marawi, Sudan. Qatar called on all parties to immediately cease hostilities, exercise maximum restraint, engage in common sense, prioritize civilian interests and take civilians into account in the outcome of the conflict.
Qatar also expressed its hope that all parties would pursue dialogue and peaceful avenues to overcome their differences...

4. The view of Qatar on the problem of the grand Ethiopian Renaissance dam:

Egyptian President Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus criticizes Qatar's stance on the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD). Mostafa Madbouli, who praised Qatar's role in supporting Egypt and Sudan on the GERD issue. Speaking to Qatar News Agency (QNA) on Tuesday, Madbouli, who is part of an official visit to Doha, said Qatar attached importance at different times to Egypt, Sudan and all Blue Basin countries finding a solution a "just, balanced and binding" one that serves the interests of all. Qatar also urged all parties to refrain from unilateral actions that could harm other Blue Basin countries.

Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulrahman Al Thani, Qatar's Minister of Foreign Affairs, announced in mid-June 2021 a unified Arab stance regarding the GERD crisis in Ethiopia, calling on the parties involved to avoid unilateral measures and instead seek a comprehensive and fair resolution.

The Qatari Minister, speaking in joint press statements alongside Ahmed Aboul Gheit, the Secretary-General of the Arab League, after the meetings of Arab foreign ministers held in Doha, stressed that "all countries have affirmed the necessity of the engagement of all parties in good faith negotiations to urgently reach a fair, balanced, and binding agreement on the GERD that serves the common interests of Egypt, Sudan, and all Nile Basin countries."

Regarding the potential for Qatar to play a mediating role in the GERD issue, this possibility was not ruled out. The understanding is that Qatar possesses previous experience in regional and international mediation efforts.

5. The view of Qatar in the secession of South Sudan from Sudan:

Sudanese Finance Minister Badr Al-Din Mahmoud said Qatar played an important economic role in Sudan. Qatar was one of the first countries to support the Sudanese economy after the break-up of South Sudan and the loss of foreign exchange Qatar provided investments that restored confidence in the economy, and actively supported ongoing economic growth pressures, and highlighted Qatar's support for peace, stability, development, and investment in Sudan. He said the economy has benefited from Qatari investments in the country, which cover all sectors of the economy including agriculture, infrastructure, real estate, livestock, minerals, banking, oil, infrastructure, and services.
